About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at the University of Missouri working in the Digital Biology Lab. My research sits at the intersection of large language models, biomedical text and figure mining, and knowledge graph construction. I am particularly interested in building AI systems that can robustly extract and organize biological knowledge to support clinicians and researchers.

Research & Interests

A snapshot of topics I focus on:

  • Prompt engineering and optimization for LLMs in biomedical and clinical domains.
  • Retrieval-augmented generation over scientific literature and pathway figures.
  • Mining gene–disease and gene–drug relationships for downstream knowledge graphs.
  • AI methods that support clinical decision making and biological discovery.

Methods & tools I frequently use:
