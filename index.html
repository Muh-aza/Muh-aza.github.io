I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at the University of Missouri and a member of the Digital Biology Lab. My research lies at the intersection of large language models, biomedical text and figure mining, and knowledge graph construction. I am particularly interested in prompt optimization and retrieval-augmented generation for extracting gene–disease and gene–drug relationships to support biomedical discovery and clinical decision making.

News

  • [2025] Manuscripts on prompt optimization and retrieval-augmented gene interaction mining are under review, including a book chapter in AI-Driven Drug Design (Springer).
  • [2024] Papers accepted at IEEE BIBM and IEEE MedAI on AI chatbots for biological pathway curation and gene name recognition from pathway figures.
  • [2023] Published work on network link prediction, software defect prediction, and blockchain applications in several international journals.
