I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at the University of Missouri and a member of the Digital Biology Lab. My research is at the intersection of large language models, biomedical text and figure mining, and knowledge graph construction. I am particularly interested in designing robust prompt and retrieval strategies for extracting gene–disease and gene–drug relationships, and in building AI systems that support biomedical discovery and clinical decision making.

News

  • [2025] Several new works on prompt optimization and LLMs for pathway figures are under review, including a chapter in AI-Driven Drug Design (Springer).
  • [2024] Papers accepted at IEEE BIBM and MedAI on AI chatbots for pathway curation and gene name recognition in pathway figures.
  • [2023] Published work on link prediction, software defect prediction, and blockchain applications in multiple international journals.
